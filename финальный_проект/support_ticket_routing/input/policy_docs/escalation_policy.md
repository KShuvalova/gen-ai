# Escalation policy

Escalation is required when at least one of the following conditions is true:

1. Priority is high or urgent.
2. The ticket is already marked as escalated.
3. SLA is breached.
4. The customer cannot access an account and previous attempts failed.
5. Payment was deducted but the transaction failed.
6. The issue affects a business-critical workflow.
7. The ticket has high complexity or multiple previous tickets.

Escalated tickets must include:
- reason for escalation
- responsible department
- evidence quote from the ticket
- recommended next action
- policy sources used by the system

Escalation must not be based on assumptions. If there is no evidence of urgency, the system should not mark the ticket as escalated.
